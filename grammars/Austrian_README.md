# Comprehensive Austrian German Grammar (Österreichisches Deutsch)

This grammar provides extensive coverage of Austrian German as spoken and written in Austria, including Viennese and regional variations.

## Grammar Size & Scope

- **500+ lines** of comprehensive grammar rules
- Production-ready for Austrian German NLP applications
- Complete Standard German grammar PLUS Austrian-specific features
- Austrian vocabulary, expressions, and cultural terms

## Austrian-Specific Features

### 1. Austrian Vocabulary

#### Culinary Terms
Austrian German has many distinctive food-related terms:
- **Paradeiser** (tomato) vs. German *Tomate*
- **Erdapfel** (potato) vs. German *Kartoffel*
- **Obers/Schlagobers** (cream/whipped cream) vs. German *Sahne/Schlagsahne*
- **Marille** (apricot) vs. German *Aprikose*
- **Karfiol** (cauliflower) vs. German *Blumenkohl*
- **Fisole** (green bean) vs. German *grüne Bohne*
- **Ribisel** (currant) vs. German *Johannisbeere*
- **Kren** (horseradish) vs. German *Meerrettich*
- **Topfen** (quark) vs. German *Quark*
- **Faschiertes** (ground meat) vs. German *Hackfleisch*
- **Palatschinke** (pancake/crêpe) vs. German *Pfannkuchen*
- **Vogerlsalat** (lamb's lettuce) vs. German *Feldsalat*
- **Schwammerl** (mushroom) vs. German *Pilz*
- **Semmel** (bread roll) vs. German *Brötchen*
- **Sackerl** (small bag) vs. German *Tüte*
- **Jause** (snack) - no direct German equivalent

#### General Vocabulary
- **Sessel** (chair) vs. German *Stuhl*
- **Kasten** (cupboard/closet) vs. German *Schrank*
- **Rauchfang** (chimney) vs. German *Schornstein*
- **Polster** (pillow) vs. German *Kissen*
- **Mistkübel** (trash can) vs. German *Mülleimer*
- **Plafond** (ceiling) vs. German *Decke*
- **Gelse** (mosquito) vs. German *Mücke*

#### Administrative Terms
- **Matura** (school-leaving exam) vs. German *Abitur*
- **Magistrat** (city administration in Vienna)
- **Bundesland/Länder** (federal state)

### 2. Austrian Temporal Expressions

- **heuer** (this year) vs. German *dieses Jahr*
- **heurig** (of this year, as adjective)
- **Jänner** (January) vs. German *Januar*
- **Feber** (February) vs. German *Februar*

### 3. Austrian Greetings and Formulas

- **Grüß Gott** - Traditional Austrian/Bavarian greeting
- **Servus** - Informal hello/goodbye (Vienna and surroundings)
- **Pfiat di / Pfiat eich** - Informal goodbye (singular/plural)
- **Baba** - Very informal goodbye
- **Habedere / Habe die Ehre** - Polite greeting
- **Küss die Hand** - Very polite/formal greeting (somewhat dated)

### 4. Austrian Diminutives

Austrian German extensively uses diminutives with **-erl**, **-l**, or **-n** suffixes:
- **Sackerl** (little bag)
- **Kipferl** (crescent roll)
- **Nachtkastl** (bedside table)
- **Schwammerl** (mushroom - already diminutive)
- **Henderl** (chicken)

### 5. Austrian Modal Particles

Austrian German uses specific modal particles:
- **halt** - emphasizing acceptance of a fact
- **eh** - "anyway," "already"
- **fei** - emphasis (regional, Bavaria/Austria border)
- **gell/gel** - tag question particle

## Core German Grammar Features

All standard German grammar features are included:

- Four-case system (nominative, accusative, dative, genitive)
- Three genders (masculine, feminine, neuter)
- V2 word order in main clauses
- Verb bracket (Satzklammer)
- Verb-final order in subordinate clauses
- Strong, weak, and mixed adjective declension
- Modal verbs: können, müssen, dürfen, sollen, wollen, mögen
- All standard tenses: present, past, perfect, pluperfect, future
- Separable prefix verbs
- Reflexive verbs
- Passive voice
- Compound nouns

## Regional Variations

This grammar primarily covers:
- **Vienna (Wien)** - The capital, setting standard for Austrian German
- **Eastern Austria** - Lower Austria, Burgenland
- **General Austrian** - Accepted across all of Austria

Note: Highly regional dialects (Tyrolean, Vorarlberg, Styrian) have more divergent features that would require separate dialect grammars.

### Austrian Culinary
```
Ich esse gerne Paradeiser.
```

### Austrian Temporal
```
Heuer fahre ich nach Wien.
Im Jänner ist es kalt.
Der heurige Wein ist ausgezeichnet.
```

### Austrian General
```
Der Sessel steht in der Ecke.
Der Kasten ist voll.
Die Gelsen sind lästig im Sommer.
```

### With Modal Particles
```
Das ist halt so. (That's just how it is)
Ich weiß es eh. (I know it anyway)
Das ist fei wichtig. (That's really important - regional)
Du kommst doch, gell? (You're coming, right?)
```
