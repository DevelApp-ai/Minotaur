# Comprehensive French Language Grammar

This grammar provides extensive coverage of French language structures for natural language processing applications.

## Grammar Size & Scope

- **750+ lines** of comprehensive grammar rules
- **Production-ready** for French language processing
- **Context-sensitive** agreement rules for gender, number, and person

## Core Features

### 1. Three Verb Groups

French verbs are organized into three main conjugation groups:

#### First Group (-er verbs) - Regular
- **parler** (to speak): je parle, tu parles, il parle, nous parlons, vous parlez, ils parlent
- Represents 90% of French verbs
- Completely regular conjugation pattern

#### Second Group (-ir verbs with -issons) - Regular
- **finir** (to finish): je finis, tu finis, il finit, nous finissons, vous finissez, ils finissent
- Regular conjugation with -iss- infix in plural forms

#### Third Group - Irregular
- All other verbs including -re, -oir, and irregular -ir verbs
- Includes most common irregular verbs

### 2. Gender System

All French nouns have grammatical gender:

- **Masculine**: le livre (the book), un homme (a man)
- **Feminine**: la table (the table), une femme (a woman)

Gender affects:
- Articles (le/la/les, un/une/des)
- Adjectives (grand/grande, beau/belle)
- Past participles (allé/allée)
- Pronouns (il/elle, celui/celle)

### 3. Article System

#### Definite Articles
- Masculine singular: **le** (le livre)
- Feminine singular: **la** (la table)
- Before vowel: **l'** (l'homme, l'eau)
- Plural: **les** (les livres, les tables)

#### Indefinite Articles
- Masculine singular: **un**
- Feminine singular: **une**
- Plural: **des**

#### Partitive Articles
- Masculine: **du** (du pain - some bread)
- Feminine: **de la** (de la viande - some meat)
- Before vowel: **de l'**
- Plural: **des**

### 4. Tense System

#### Present Tense (Présent)
- Current actions or states
- Habitual actions
- General truths

#### Imperfect (Imparfait)
- Ongoing past actions
- Descriptions in the past
- Habitual past actions

#### Passé Composé
- Completed past actions
- Formed with auxiliary (avoir/être) + past participle
- Most common past tense in spoken French

**Agreement**: With être, past participle agrees with subject:
- Il est allé (he went)
- Elle est allée (she went)
- Ils sont allés (they masc. went)
- Elles sont allées (they fem. went)

#### Future (Futur Simple)
- Future actions
- Formed with infinitive + endings

#### Conditional (Conditionnel)
- Hypothetical situations
- Polite requests

### 5. Subjunctive Mood (Subjonctif)

Used after certain expressions and conjunctions:

**After verbs of**:
- Desire: vouloir que, souhaiter que
- Emotion: avoir peur que, être content que
- Doubt: douter que
- Necessity: il faut que, il est nécessaire que

**After conjunctions**:
- bien que (although)
- pour que (so that)
- avant que (before)
- à moins que (unless)

### 6. Adjective Agreement

Adjectives agree with nouns in gender and number:

**Masculine singular**: grand
**Feminine singular**: grande (add -e)
**Masculine plural**: grands (add -s)
**Feminine plural**: grandes (add -es)

**Irregular adjectives**:
- beau/belle/beaux/belles (beautiful)
- nouveau/nouvelle/nouveaux/nouvelles (new)
- vieux/vieille/vieux/vieilles (old)

**Position**:
- Most adjectives follow the noun: une voiture rouge
- Some common short adjectives precede: un grand homme, une belle femme

### 7. Adjective Comparison

#### Comparative
- **Plus...que**: plus grand que (taller than)
- **Moins...que**: moins grand que (less tall than)
- **Aussi...que**: aussi grand que (as tall as)

**Irregular**: bon → meilleur (good → better)

#### Superlative
- **Le/la/les plus**: le plus grand (the tallest)
- **Le/la/les moins**: le moins grand (the least tall)

**Irregular**: bon → le meilleur (good → the best)

### 8. Pronoun System

#### Subject Pronouns
- je (I), tu (you sing.), il/elle/on (he/she/one)
- nous (we), vous (you pl./formal), ils/elles (they)

#### Object Pronouns
**Direct**: me, te, le/la, nous, vous, les
**Indirect**: me, te, lui, nous, vous, leur

**Order before verb**: me/te/se/nous/vous + le/la/les + lui/leur + y + en

#### Reflexive Pronouns
Used with pronominal verbs:
- me, te, se, nous, vous, se

#### Adverbial Pronouns
- **y**: replaces à + place/thing
- **en**: replaces de + thing, partitive

### 9. Negation Patterns

French negation uses two parts around the verb:

**ne...pas**: not
- Je ne parle pas (I don't speak)

**ne...plus**: no longer
- Je ne fume plus (I no longer smoke)

**ne...jamais**: never
- Je ne vais jamais là (I never go there)

**ne...rien**: nothing
- Je ne vois rien (I see nothing)

**ne...personne**: nobody
- Je ne vois personne (I see nobody)

**ne...aucun(e)**: no, none
- Je n'ai aucune idée (I have no idea)

### 10. Interrogative Forms

Three ways to form yes/no questions:

**Intonation**: Tu viens? (spoken)
**Est-ce que**: Est-ce que tu viens?
**Inversion**: Viens-tu?

**Wh-questions**:
- Qui? (who), Que/Quoi? (what), Où? (where)
- Quand? (when), Comment? (how), Pourquoi? (why)
- Combien? (how much/many)
- Quel/Quelle/Quels/Quelles? (which)

### 11. Word Order

**Standard**: Subject + Verb + Object (SVO)
- Je mange une pomme

**With object pronouns**: Subject + Pronoun(s) + Verb
- Je la mange (I eat it)
- Je lui parle (I speak to him/her)

**In compound tenses**: Subject + Pronoun + Auxiliary + Past Participle
- Je l'ai mangée (I ate it)

**With negation**: ne + pronoun + verb + pas
- Je ne la mange pas

### 12. Pronominal Verbs

Verbs used with reflexive pronouns:

**Reflexive**: se laver (to wash oneself)
- Je me lave, tu te laves, il se lave

**Reciprocal**: se parler (to speak to each other)
- Nous nous parlons, vous vous parlez, ils se parlent

**Idiomatic**: se promener (to go for a walk), s'appeler (to be called)

### 13. Prepositions

Common prepositions:
- **à**: to, at, in
- **de**: of, from
- **en**: in, to (with countries/materials)
- **dans**: in, inside
- **sur**: on, upon
- **sous**: under
- **avec**: with
- **sans**: without
- **pour**: for
- **par**: by, through
- **chez**: at (someone's place)
- **depuis**: since, for (time)

**Contractions**:
- à + le = au
- à + les = aux
- de + le = du
- de + les = des

## Context-Sensitive Validation

The grammar includes validation rules for:

1. **Article-noun gender agreement**: Articles must match noun gender
2. **Article-noun number agreement**: Articles must match noun number
3. **Adjective-noun agreements**: Gender and number must align
4. **Subject-verb agreement**: Number and person must match
5. **Past participle agreement**: With être, participle agrees with subject
6. **Negation completeness**: ne requires second part (pas, jamais, etc.)

## Technical Implementation

- **Format**: CEBNF (Context-Enhanced Backus-Naur Form)
- **Token splitting**: Space-based for natural language
- **Context attributes**:
  - @GENDER[masculine|feminine]
  - @NUMBER[singular|plural]
  - @PERSON[1|2|3]
  - @CASE[nominative|accusative|dative]
  - @GROUP[1|2|3]
  - @TENSE[present|imperfect|future|perfect]
  - @MOOD[indicative|subjunctive|imperative|conditional]
  - @DEGREE[positive|comparative|superlative]
  - @AGREEMENT (for adjectives)
  - @IRREGULAR (marker for irregular forms)
  - @REFLEXIVE (marker for reflexive pronouns)
