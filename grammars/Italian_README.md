# Comprehensive Italian Language Grammar

This grammar provides extensive coverage of Italian language structures for natural language processing applications.

## Grammar Size & Scope

- **500+ lines** of comprehensive grammar rules
- **Production-ready** for Italian language processing
- **Context-sensitive** agreement rules for gender, number, and person

## Core Features

### 1. Three Verb Groups

Italian verbs are organized into three main conjugation groups:

#### First Group (-are verbs) - Regular
- **parlare** (to speak): parlo, parli, parla, parliamo, parlate, parlano
- Most common verb group
- Regular conjugation pattern

#### Second Group (-ere verbs) - Regular
- **credere** (to believe): credo, credi, crede, crediamo, credete, credono
- Regular conjugation pattern

#### Third Group (-ire verbs) - Two patterns
- **dormire** (to sleep): dormo, dormi, dorme, dormiamo, dormite, dormono
- **finire** (to finish): finisco, finisci, finisce, finiamo, finite, finiscono (-isc- infix)

### 2. Gender System

All Italian nouns have grammatical gender:

- **Masculine**: il libro (the book), un uomo (a man)
  - Usually end in -o (singular) or -i (plural)
- **Feminine**: la tavola (the table), una donna (a woman)
  - Usually end in -a (singular) or -e (plural)

Gender affects:
- Articles (il/la/i/le, un/una/uno/un')
- Adjectives (alto/alta, bello/bella)
- Past participles (andato/andata)
- Pronouns (lui/lei, quello/quella)

### 3. Article System

#### Definite Articles
- Masculine singular: **il** (il libro), **lo** (before z, s+consonant, gn, ps), **l'** (before vowel)
- Feminine singular: **la** (la tavola), **l'** (before vowel)
- Masculine plural: **i** (i libri), **gli** (gli uomini)
- Feminine plural: **le** (le tavole)

#### Indefinite Articles
- Masculine: **un** (un libro), **uno** (uno studente)
- Feminine: **una** (una tavola), **un'** (un'amica - before vowel)

#### Prepositional Articles (Contractions)
Prepositions combine with definite articles:
- **a + il = al**, **a + lo = allo**, **a + la = alla**
- **di + il = del**, **di + lo = dello**, **di + la = della**
- **da + il = dal**, **in + il = nel**, **su + il = sul**

### 4. Tense System

#### Present Tense (Presente)
- Current actions or states
- Habitual actions
- General truths

#### Imperfect (Imperfetto)
- Ongoing past actions
- Descriptions in the past
- Habitual past actions

#### Passato Prossimo
- Completed past actions
- Formed with auxiliary (avere/essere) + past participle
- Most common past tense

**Agreement**: With essere, past participle agrees with subject:
- Lui è andato (he went)
- Lei è andata (she went)
- Loro sono andati (they masc. went)
- Loro sono andate (they fem. went)

#### Future (Futuro Semplice)
- Future actions
- Formed with infinitive (minus -e) + endings

#### Conditional (Condizionale)
- Hypothetical situations
- Polite requests

### 5. Subjunctive Mood (Congiuntivo)

Used after certain expressions and conjunctions:

**After verbs of**:
- Desire: volere che, sperare che
- Emotion: temere che, essere contento che
- Doubt: dubitare che
- Necessity: bisogna che, è necessario che

**After conjunctions**:
- benché (although)
- affinché (so that)
- prima che (before)
- senza che (without)

### 6. Adjective Agreement

Adjectives agree with nouns in gender and number:

**Masculine singular**: alto (tall)
**Feminine singular**: alta
**Masculine plural**: alti
**Feminine plural**: alte

**Common patterns**:
- Adjectives in -o: alto/alta/alti/alte
- Adjectives in -e: grande/grande/grandi/grandi

**Position**:
- Most adjectives follow the noun: una macchina rossa
- Some common adjectives precede: un bel ragazzo, una grande casa

### 7. Adjective Comparison

#### Comparative
- **Più...di/che**: più alto di (taller than)
- **Meno...di/che**: meno alto di (less tall than)
- **Tanto...quanto**: tanto alto quanto (as tall as)

**Irregular**: buono → migliore (good → better), cattivo → peggiore (bad → worse)

#### Superlative

**Relative**: 
- **Il/la/i/le più**: il più alto (the tallest)
- **Il/la/i/le meno**: il meno alto (the least tall)

**Irregular**: buono → il migliore/ottimo, cattivo → il peggiore/pessimo

**Absolute superlative**: Add -issimo/-issima/-issimi/-issime
- bello → bellissimo (very beautiful)
- alto → altissimo (very tall)

### 8. Pronoun System

#### Subject Pronouns
- io (I), tu (you sing.), lui/lei (he/she)
- noi (we), voi (you pl.), loro (they)
- Lei (formal you)

**Note**: Subject pronouns often omitted in Italian

#### Object Pronouns
**Direct**: mi, ti, lo/la (La), ci, vi, li/le (Li/Le)
**Indirect**: mi, ti, gli/le (Le), ci, vi, loro

**Order before verb**: indirect + direct + ne
- Me lo dai (You give it to me)

#### Reflexive Pronouns
Used with reflexive verbs:
- mi, ti, si, ci, vi, si

#### Adverbial Pronouns
- **ne**: replaces di + something, partitive
- **ci**: replaces a/in + place

### 9. Negation Patterns

**non**: not
- Non parlo (I don't speak)

**non...mai**: never
- Non vado mai là (I never go there)

**non...più**: no longer
- Non fumo più (I no longer smoke)

**non...niente/nulla**: nothing
- Non vedo niente (I see nothing)

**non...nessuno**: nobody
- Non vedo nessuno (I see nobody)

**non...ancora**: not yet
- Non è ancora arrivato (He hasn't arrived yet)

### 10. Interrogative Forms

**Intonation**: Simply raise voice at end
- Parli italiano? (spoken)

**Wh-questions**:
- Chi? (who), Che/Cosa/Che cosa? (what), Dove? (where)
- Quando? (when), Come? (how), Perché? (why)
- Quanto/a/i/e? (how much/many)
- Quale/Quali? (which)

### 11. Word Order

**Standard**: Subject + Verb + Object (SVO)
- Io mangio una mela

**With object pronouns**: Subject + Pronoun(s) + Verb
- La mangio (I eat it)
- Gli parlo (I speak to him)

**In compound tenses**: Subject + Pronoun + Auxiliary + Past Participle
- L'ho mangiata (I ate it - fem.)

**With negation**: Subject + non + pronoun + verb
- Non la mangio (I don't eat it)

### 12. Reflexive Verbs

Verbs used with reflexive pronouns:

**Reflexive**: lavarsi (to wash oneself)
- Mi lavo, ti lavi, si lava, ci laviamo, vi lavate, si lavano

**Reciprocal**: parlarsi (to speak to each other)
- Ci parliamo, vi parlate, si parlano

**Idiomatic**: alzarsi (to get up), chiamarsi (to be called)

### 13. Prepositions

Common prepositions:
- **a**: to, at, in
- **di**: of, from, about
- **da**: from, by, since
- **in**: in, to
- **con**: with
- **su**: on
- **per**: for, through
- **tra/fra**: between, among
- **verso**: toward
- **durante**: during
- **senza**: without

### 14. Modal Verbs

**dovere** (must, to have to): devo, devi, deve, dobbiamo, dovete, devono

**potere** (can, to be able): posso, puoi, può, possiamo, potete, possono

**volere** (to want): voglio, vuoi, vuole, vogliamo, volete, vogliono

**sapere** (to know how): so, sai, sa, sappiamo, sapete, sanno

Used with infinitive:
- Posso parlare (I can speak)
- Voglio mangiare (I want to eat)

## Context-Sensitive Validation

The grammar includes validation rules for:

1. **Article-noun gender agreement**: Articles must match noun gender
2. **Article-noun number agreement**: Articles must match noun number
3. **Adjective-noun agreements**: Gender and number must align
4. **Subject-verb agreement**: Number and person must match
5. **Past participle agreement**: With essere, participle agrees with subject

## Technical Implementation

- **Format**: CEBNF (Context-Enhanced Backus-Naur Form)
- **Token splitting**: Space-based for natural language
- **Context attributes**:
  - @GENDER[masculine|feminine]
  - @NUMBER[singular|plural]
  - @PERSON[1|2|3]
  - @CASE[nominative|accusative|dative]
  - @GROUP[1|2|3]
  - @TENSE[present|imperfect|future|perfect]
  - @MOOD[indicative|subjunctive|imperative|conditional]
  - @DEGREE[positive|comparative|superlative]
  - @AGREEMENT (for adjectives)
  - @IRREGULAR (marker for irregular forms)
  - @REFLEXIVE (marker for reflexive pronouns)
