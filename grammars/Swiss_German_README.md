# Comprehensive Swiss German Grammar (Schweizerdeutsch)

This grammar provides extensive coverage of Swiss German as spoken and written in Switzerland, particularly the written standard form used in formal contexts.

## Grammar Size & Scope

- **490+ lines** of comprehensive grammar rules
- Production-ready for Swiss German NLP applications
- Complete Standard German grammar with Swiss-specific features
- Swiss vocabulary, expressions, and loanwords

## Swiss German Characteristics

### 1. Tense Usage

**Key Difference**: Swiss German strongly prefers the **perfect tense** over the preterite (simple past) in spoken language:

- **German**: "Ich ging nach Hause" (preterite)
- **Swiss**: "Ich bin nach Hause gegangen" (perfect - preferred)

The preterite is used mainly in formal writing and for modal verbs and haben/sein.

### 2. Swiss-Specific Vocabulary

#### Transportation and Infrastructure
- **Velo** (bicycle) vs. German *Fahrrad*
- **Trottoir** (sidewalk - from French) vs. German *Bürgersteig*
- **Tram/Tramway** (streetcar) - same in Swiss and German, but more common in Switzerland
- **Perron** (train platform - from French) vs. German *Bahnsteig*
- **Billette/Billett** (ticket - from French) vs. German *Fahrkarte*
- **Töffli** (moped) - uniquely Swiss
- **parkieren** (to park) vs. German *parken*

#### Food and Daily Life
- **Rüebli** (carrot) vs. German *Karotte/Möhre*
- **Weggli** (bread roll) vs. German *Brötchen*
- **Brötli** (small bread roll) - diminutive
- **Gipfeli** (croissant) vs. German *Croissant*/Hörnchen*
- **Anken** (butter) vs. German *Butter*
- **Zückerli** (candy) vs. German *Bonbon*
- **Guetzli** (cookie) vs. German *Keks*
- **Rösti** (Swiss potato dish) - uniquely Swiss
- **Poulet** (chicken - from French) vs. German *Hähnchen*

#### General Vocabulary
- **Natel** (mobile phone - from "Nationales Autotelefon") vs. German *Handy*
- **Estrich** (attic) vs. German *Dachboden*
- **Putzen** can mean "to clean" (same) but also "to fall" (Swiss-specific)
- **Coiffeur/Coiffeuse** (hairdresser - from French) vs. German *Friseur/Friseurin*
- **Portemonnaie** (wallet - from French) vs. German *Geldbörse*
- **Occasion** (used/second-hand - from French) vs. German *gebraucht*
- **Camion** (truck - from French) vs. German *Lastwagen*

### 3. French Influence

Due to Switzerland's multilingual nature, Swiss German incorporates many French loanwords, especially in:
- **Services**: Coiffeur, Portemonnaie
- **Transportation**: Trottoir, Perron, Billette
- **Food**: Poulet, Cordon bleu, Filet
- **General**: Merci (thank you - often used alongside Danke)

### 4. Swiss Greetings

- **Grüezi** - Formal Swiss German greeting (from "Gott grüß Euch")
- **Grüessech** - Plural form of Grüezi
- **Guete Morge** - Good morning (Swiss form)
- **Guete Tag** - Good day (Swiss form)
- **Guete Aabig** - Good evening (Swiss form)
- **Uf Widerluege** - Goodbye (Swiss form - "until we see each other again")
- **Adé/Adieu** - Goodbye (from French)
- **Ciao/Tschau** - Informal goodbye (from Italian)

### 5. Diminutives with -li

Swiss German extensively uses the **-li** suffix for diminutives:
- **Zückerli** (candy - from Zucker)
- **Guetzli** (cookie - from Guetz)
- **Brötli** (small bread roll - from Brot)
- **Hündli** (little dog - from Hund)
- **Chätzli** (little cat - from Chatz/Katze)

This contrasts with German's **-chen** or **-lein** suffixes.

### 6. Administrative Terms

Swiss federal structure:
- **Kanton/Kantone** (canton - Swiss federal state)
- **Gemeinde** (municipality)
- **Bundesrat** (Federal Council - Swiss executive)
- **Nationalrat** (National Council - Swiss lower house)
- **Ständerat** (Council of States - Swiss upper house)

## Core German Grammar Features

All standard German grammar features are included:

- Four-case system (nominative, accusative, dative, genitive)
- Three genders (masculine, feminine, neuter)
- V2 word order in main clauses
- Verb bracket (Satzklammer)
- Verb-final order in subordinate clauses
- Strong, weak, and mixed adjective declension
- Modal verbs: können, müssen, dürfen, sollen, wollen, mögen
- Tenses: present, perfect (preferred), pluperfect, future
- Separable prefix verbs
- Reflexive verbs
- Passive voice
- Compound nouns

## Swiss German Dialects

Note: This grammar covers **written Swiss German** (Schweizer Hochdeutsch), which is the standard written form used in Switzerland. Spoken Swiss German dialects (e.g., Züritüütsch, Bärndütsch, Baseldütsch) are significantly different and would require separate grammars for comprehensive coverage.

### Swiss Food
```
Die Rüebli sind frisch.
Ich hätte gerne zwei Weggli.
Das Gipfeli schmeckt gut.
Der Anken ist bio.
Möchtest du ein Zückerli?

### Perfect Tense Preference
```
Ich bin gestern nach Zürich gefahren. (not: fuhr)
Sie hat das Buch gelesen. (not: las)
Wir sind ins Kino gegangen. (not: gingen)
```

```
