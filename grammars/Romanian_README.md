# Romanian Language Grammar

Comprehensive Romanian grammar covering unique Romance language features including the enclitic definite article system.

## Grammar Size & Scope

- **440+ lines** of comprehensive grammar rules
- **5 grammatical cases**: Nominative, Accusative, Genitive-Dative (merged), Vocative
- **4 verb conjugation groups**: -a, -ea, -e, -i/-î
- **3 genders**: Masculine, Feminine, Neuter (with unique pattern)
- **Enclitic definite articles**: Suffixed to nouns (unique to Romanian among Romance languages)

## Unique Romanian Features

### 1. Definite Article Suffixes (Enclitic Articles)
Unlike other Romance languages, Romanian attaches the definite article to the end of the noun:
- **Masculine singular**: băiat → băiatul (the boy)
- **Feminine singular**: carte → cartea (the book)
- **Neuter singular**: scaun → scaunul (the chair)
- **Masculine plural**: băieți → băieții (the boys)
- **Feminine plural**: cărți → cărțile (the books)

### 2. Neuter Gender Pattern
Neuter nouns behave uniquely:
- **Singular**: Masculine forms (scaunul bun - the good chair)
- **Plural**: Feminine forms (scaunele bune - the good chairs)

### 3. Genitive-Dative Case Merger
Romanian merges genitive and dative cases into a single form:
- **Genitive/Dative singular**: al băiatului, băiatului
- **Genitive/Dative plural**: al băieților, băieților

### 4. Clitic Doubling
Indirect objects can be doubled with a clitic pronoun:
- **Îi dau cartea Mariei** (lit. "To-her I-give the-book to-Maria")

### 5. Differential Object Marking
Animate/definite direct objects take preposition "pe":
- **Văd pe Maria** (I see Maria) vs **Văd cartea** (I see the book)

## Core Features

### Four Conjugation Groups
- **I (-a)**: lucra (to work) → lucrez, lucrezi, lucrează
- **II (-ea)**: vedea (to see) → văd, vezi, vede
- **III (-e)**: face (to do/make) → fac, faci, face
- **IV (-i/-î)**: iubi (to love) → iubesc, iubești, iubește

### Major Irregular Verbs
a fi (to be), a avea (to have), a face (to do/make), a vrea (to want), a putea (can), a ști (to know), a da (to give), a lua (to take), a vedea (to see), a merge (to go)

### Two Future Forms
- **Periphrastic**: voi lucra, vei lucra, va lucra
- **Subjunctive**: o să lucrez, o să lucrezi, o să lucreze

### Past Tenses
- **Perfect compus** (compound): am lucrat (I worked/have worked) - most common
- **Simple perfect**: lucrai (I worked) - literary
- **Imperfect**: lucram (I was working)
- **Pluperfect**: am fost lucrat (I had worked)
